Consent Form

PURPOSE/DESCRIPTION OF THE RESEARCH

  • You are invited to participate in a research study about the willingness of people to buy oysters with a number of different attributes. This research is being conducted by Dr. Kent Messer and Dr. Maik Kecinski at the University of Delaware.

PROCEDURES AND CONDITIONS OF SUBJECT PARTICIPATION

  • We will ask you to make a series of decisions about whether you are willing to buy a certain oyster bundle for a listed price. You can select 'YES' or 'NO' to each bundle.
  • Unless we tell you otherwise ahead of time, all the decisions you make will be kept confidential.

RISKS AND BENEFITS

  • There are no greater physical risks from participating in this study than those you would face consuming raw oysters in restaurants or at home. Some of the things we ask you to do may cause you to feel psychological discomfort, but they are not dangerous.
  • There are no substantial benefits to you from the research. By learning more about people's decision-making, we hope that the research will benefit society by helping government agencies and non-profit organizations design more effective programs and policies.

FINANCIAL CONSIDERATIONS

  • You will have the opportunity to earn money and/or oysters by participating in this study. This study will take approximately 10 minutes to complete. Any costs incurred in order to attend the experiment are your own.

CONFIDENTIALITY

  • Your participation in this study is anonymous: we will not ask you for your name, or any other information that could be used to identify you.

SUBJECT'S ASSURANCES

  • Your participation is strictly voluntary. You may refuse to participate before the study begins or withdraw at any time. Your decision to withdraw will not result in loss of the compensation or affect you in any other way.

QUESTIONS AND CONTACTS

  • If you have questions about your rights as a research participant or wish to report a research-related injury, please contact the Institutional Review Board Office, University of Delaware: (302) 831-4400.
